Research

Statistical Analysis of Master Regulators Underlying Skin Aging.

A computational biology project examining how gene activity and cell behavior differ between young and aged human skin.

Studying the biology of skin aging.

I analyzed single-cell RNA sequencing data from young and aged human skin to identify changes in cell populations, biological pathways, and transcription-factor activity.

Dates2025–Present
DataSingle-cell RNA sequencing
MethodsClustering and pathway analysis
FocusSkin aging and gene regulation
Related activityPittsburgh research ↗

Analyzing individual cells

I worked with single-cell RNA sequencing data to separate skin cells into groups and compare patterns of gene expression between younger and older samples.

Identifying biological changes

I used clustering and pathway-enrichment analysis to study which cellular processes become more or less active with age.

Studying gene regulation

I examined transcription-factor activity to identify potential master regulators associated with age-related changes in human skin.

Research outcomes

This work contributed to research software for cell-level biological analysis and earned third place in Health and Medicine at the Pittsburgh Regional Science and Engineering Fair and first place in Biology at the Pittsburgh Junior Academy of Science.

A research paper based on this work is currently in progress. More information coming soon :)
